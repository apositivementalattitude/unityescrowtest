Business "Bulk" Sale

A business sale or "bulk" sale Escrow is processed when a business is being sold to a Buyer from the Seller who is currently operating the business. Process of processing a bulk escrow is different from the escrow involving real property and requires a specially trained and experienced Escrow Officer. Many years of experience is a must to work in our office and to process the bulk escrow.

  • Family owned business
  • Franchise Business
  • ABC License transfer
  • Sales of: Retail Store, Restaurant, Coin Laundry, Market, Car Wash, Gas Station, Dry Cleaners, etc.

Requirements to "open" a Bulk Sale escrow

  • A copy of the Purchase Contract, all counter Offers and Addendums signed by all parties.
  • Name, address, phone number and e-mail address of the Buyer.
  • Name, address, phone number and e-mail address of the Seller
  • unless Seller is represented by a real estate agent in which case the agent’s information is required. Legal name being used for the purchase. Final acceptance date. A list of contingencies (i.e. approval for a new loan, selling another business).

Accountability and Safety

The benefit of using an independent escrow company is the fact that we are licensed by the State of California Department of Business Oversight (DBO) as well a member of the Escrow Agents Fidelity Corporation, whereas other companies aligned with title companies are only licensed through the Department of Insurance. Our company is kept accountable through regular financial audits to ensure compliance with the DBO., and employees are required to pass background check governed by the EAFC through live scan fingerprinting required by the FBI and DOJ. As a member of the EAFC, consumers are protected through a $5 million fidelity bond.

How do I open Escrow?

Call or Email our Escrow Officer and ask to "open escrow" or for an OPENING SHEET, that 's all it takes. Once we receive the information we guide you through the Escrow Process. Have the Property Address and Names ready.

What is Escrow?

A legal procedure that involves the depositing/disbursing of funds, deeds, and other documents to a neutral, unbiased third party (Escrow Holder) until all requirements and conditions are met to officially transfer real property. In other words, we make sure there’s no “phishy” business going on in the entire process of purchasing/selling or refinancing your home!

The two magic phrases to look for in escrow are:

"Loan has been funded!" and “Recording has been confirmed!”

Ommitting or concealing any liens and/or judgments will only delay and complicate the scrow process.

Remember, anything recorded is considered a public record!

You must bring a valid form of photo ID

All parties must be present during loan document signing... and maybe a pair of eyeglasses couldn't hurt.
